What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?
 Git creates some metadata to enable file tracking.
How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? what value do you think it offers?
 it sets aside files that will be committed to the repository.
How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?
 when a logical change has been made place the relevant files to the staging area to separate them from the files that are not ready to be committed.
What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organize? How would branches help?
 Working on different major changes, multiple people working on different major changes
How do diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?
 They help you see on what commit the project branched off in.
What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do?
 A combined branch that has the changes and commit history of the two parent branches. To display creation order and point of origin.
What are the pros and cons of Git's automatic merging vs. always doing merges manually?
 Automatic merging can lead to a merge that doesnt make sense logically or both branches are incomplete. It may ganruntee the most up to date code between branches. Manual merges may take longer to do. Issue can be worked out and merges can be more meaningful.
